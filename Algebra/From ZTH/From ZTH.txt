Rank 4 curves. From ZTH.
(Ref)2023.Simon Aranda

Let the surface:  "z^2 = x^2 + xy + y^2"

Take x,y,z all positive, y<x,
and "z" some fixed prime number (1 mod 6).
The equation always has "exactly one" integral solution (x,y).
Let n= x*y*(x+y)*z.
Then the curve C[n] will (likely) have rank between 2 and 4.

Some examples z<1000
