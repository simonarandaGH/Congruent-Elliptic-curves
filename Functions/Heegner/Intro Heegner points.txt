
Heegner points
(C)2023. Simon Aranda

A Heegner point is a image of a quadratic imaginary point of the upper half-plane. The Grossâ€“Zagier Theorem describes the height in terms of a derivative of the "L-function" at the point s=1. As generator point of infinite order can generate large rational points and the heights of these points were the coefficients of a modular form of weight 3/2. 

Examples included:
01 - n prime
02 - Some integral H points
03 - Doubles of Heegner points, n<100
